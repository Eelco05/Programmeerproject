The Samnite Sanctuaries of S. Geiovanni in Galdo and Gildone, Cupa

The remains of S. Giovanni in Galdo, località Colle Rimontato, provide a good visual impression of the ancient sactuary. The Temple building represents the monumentalized phase (from the late 2nd to 1st century BC) and has been considered a 'typical' Samnite sactuary of this period. The context of the sactuary at Gildone, località Cupa, on the other hand, does not present much architecture in situ, due to interventions made in 1935-1938 during the construction of a road. A sesemblence of the sactuary of Colle Rimontanto has seemed plausible to most authors. Some of the terracotta rood decoration has been preserved, as well as relatively opulent votive gifts from a nearby deposit. In the wider area on the slopes of Montagna di Gildone several archaeological traces have been documented, but the direct context of the sanctuary in unknown.
Stek & Pelgrom 2005
